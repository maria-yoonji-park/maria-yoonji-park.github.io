Information About me

Yoonji Park is a Korean-born classical pianist who has garnered acclaim for her captivating performances and impressive musicality. Praised as a "blessed lucky pianist" by the revered pianist and musicologist Paul Badura-Skoda, Yoonji has cultivated her artistry under the tutelage of renowned pedagogues Zenon Fishbein, Alexandre Moutouzkine, and Mykola Suk.

A graduate of the Manhattan School of Music where she earned both her Bachelor and Master of Music degrees, Yoonji continued her doctoral studies at the University of Maryland and is currently completing her Doctorate at the University of Nevada, Las Vegas under the guidance of Mykola Suk.

Yoonji's captivating performances have taken her across North America, Asia, and Europe, gracing stages in Italy, France, Germany, and Portugal. Her passion for chamber music has led to engaging recitals at prestigious venues such as Greenfield Hall, Pforzheimer Hall, and David A. Rahm Hall.

Beyond her mastery of the modern piano, Yoonji possesses a deep appreciation for early music, evident in her performances of Handel's "Acis and Galatea" and Bach's "Schweigt stille, plaudert nicht" (Coffee Cantata) on the harpsichord with Maestro Kenneth Cooper.

Her commitment to artistic growth is further demonstrated by her participation in numerous masterclasses with distinguished figures like Paul Badura-Skoda, Boris Berman, and Boris Slutsky, among others, and through her involvement in esteemed music festivals such as IKIF in New York, Forum Musicae in Madrid, and Euro Music Festival in Halle.

Yoonji's dedication to music education is reflected in her inclusion on the Manhattan School of Music's qualified piano teacher list and her current position as a part-time faculty member in the music department at UNLV.
